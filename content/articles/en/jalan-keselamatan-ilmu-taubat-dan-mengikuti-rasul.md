## Introduction

A servant’s journey toward salvation in this world and the Hereafter is not a straight path without obstacles. Rather, it is a long journey filled with trials, temptations, and mistakes. The scholars and the people of spiritual discipline have described this journey as an uphill path that must pass through many difficult *‘aqabah* (steep obstacles or ascents). Each ascent has its own trial, and a servant will not reach his final goal—namely, the pleasure of Allah and salvation in the Hereafter—unless he is able to pass through these ascents properly.

Among the earliest and most important of these ascents is the *‘aqabah* of knowledge, meaning the obstacle found in seeking and understanding religious knowledge. Knowledge is not merely an additional feature in a Muslim’s life, but the primary foundation of all deeds. Without knowledge, a person will not know who his Lord is, how to worship Him, or what he must do or avoid in life. For that reason, the scholars emphasized that knowledge is the محور, the central axis, of religious life, and that all deeds revolve around it.

In the texts of the Qur’an, it becomes clear that the purpose behind the creation of the universe and of humankind is closely tied to knowledge and worship. Human beings are commanded to know Allah, understand His power, and worship Him with awareness. Thus, knowledge and worship are the two central matters that form the core of the entire Sharia. Without knowledge, worship may go in the wrong direction and may even become fruitless. On the other hand, knowledge without worship becomes only a burden and an argument against the person himself.

That is why the scholars placed knowledge as the first step in a servant’s spiritual journey. Knowledge becomes the light that illuminates the road, the guide that shows the correct direction, and the guard that prevents a person from falling into misguidance. With knowledge, a servant comes to know his obligations, recognizes the diseases of the heart, and understands the path to repentance and self-reform.

In addition to knowledge, the journey toward salvation also requires continual repentance and sincere adherence to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. Repentance cleanses the heart from the filth of sins, while following the Prophet is the sure path to the pleasure of Allah. Without repentance, the heart becomes covered by the darkness of sin. Without following the Prophet, deeds lose their direction and their value.

The text presented by the scholars in this discussion combines three major foundations of the spiritual journey: knowledge, repentance, and *ittiba‘* (following the Prophet). These three are interconnected and cannot be separated. Knowledge guides repentance, repentance purifies the heart, and following the Prophet perfects one’s deeds. By understanding these three matters, a servant may begin his journey to Allah with correct steps, a pure heart, and accepted deeds.

May Allah make us among His servants who are granted beneficial knowledge, sincere repentance, and the ability to follow His Messenger in every circumstance. And Allah knows best.

## Knowledge

In a servant’s journey toward salvation, the first ascent that must be crossed is knowledge. It is said in the passage:

> يا طالب الخلاص والعبادة عليك أولاً وفقك الله بالعلم فإنه القطب وعليه المدار  
>
> “O seeker of salvation and worship, you must begin first—may Allah grant you success—with knowledge, for knowledge is the axis, and everything revolves around it.”

The meaning of this statement is that all deeds, whether outward acts of worship or inward spiritual acts, cannot proceed correctly without knowledge. Knowledge is the center and foundation of all religious life. Just as a wheel revolves around its axis, so too do a servant’s deeds revolve around knowledge. If the axis is damaged, the entire movement becomes unstable. Likewise, if knowledge is corrupted or absent, worship cannot be sound.

It is also mentioned that knowledge and worship are two jewels that form the primary purpose of creation. For their sake, the scriptures were revealed, the messengers were sent, and even the heavens and the earth and all that is within them were created. This shows the greatness of the position of knowledge and worship in human life.

Allah ﷻ says:

> اللَّهُ الَّذِي خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَوَاتٍ وَمِنَ الْأَرْضِ مِثْلَهُنَّ… لِتَعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ  
>
> “Allah is the One who created seven heavens and of the earth the like of them… so that you may know that Allah has power over all things.” (Qur’an, At-Talaq: 12)

This verse shows that the purpose of the creation of the universe is that human beings may know the power of Allah. The phrase “so that you may know” emphasizes that knowledge—especially knowledge of tawhid—is among the primary purposes of creation.

Allah also says:

> وَمَا خَلَقْتُ الْجِنَّ وَالْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ  
>
> “I did not create jinn and humankind except that they should worship Me.” (Qur’an, Adh-Dhariyat: 56)

This verse shows that the purpose of creating humankind is worship. Thus, the purpose of creating all beings returns to two matters: knowledge and worship. For this reason, a servant should not occupy himself except with these two matters, nor spend his life except for them.

However, it is mentioned that knowledge is nobler than worship, because knowledge is the root, while worship is its fruit. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:

> فضل العالم على العابد كفضلي على أدنى رجل من أصحابي  
>
> “The superiority of the scholar over the devout worshipper is like my superiority over the lowest-ranking man among my Companions.”

In another hadith, it is mentioned:

“Looking at a scholar is more beloved to me than a year of worship with fasting by day and prayer by night.”

The meaning of these hadiths is that knowledge holds an extremely high rank. Knowledge is compared to a tree, while worship is compared to its fruit. The tree is the source and the foundation, but the fruit is the benefit that is enjoyed. The two cannot be separated. A tree without fruit provides no complete benefit, and fruit without a tree cannot exist.

For this reason, a servant must possess both knowledge and worship. Al-Hasan al-Basri رحمه الله said:

“Seek knowledge in a way that does not damage worship, and seek worship in a way that does not damage knowledge.”

After understanding that both are necessary, knowledge must still be given precedence. It is stated in the passage:

> العلم إمام العمل والعمل تابعه  
>
> “Knowledge is the leader of deeds, and deeds are its followers.”

This means that deeds cannot be correct without the knowledge that guides them. Knowledge is the leader, while deeds follow the direction shown by knowledge.

Knowledge must be given priority for two principal reasons.

The first reason is so that worship may be correct and sound. A person must know his Lord before worshipping Him. How can someone worship Allah without knowing who Allah is, what His attributes are, what is fitting for Him, and what is impossible in relation to Him? Without knowledge, a person may hold false beliefs, and then his worship will have no value before Allah.

In addition, a person must know what obligations he is required to perform and what prohibitions he is required to avoid. How can he perform prayer, fasting, and other acts of worship if he does not know their conditions, pillars, and method? A person may worship for many years, yet his worship may be invalid because it does not conform to the correct guidance, while he remains unaware of this.

The same applies to inward actions of the heart, such as reliance upon Allah, sincerity, patience, contentment, repentance, and fear of Allah. All these are obligations that must be learned. Otherwise, a person may be in a state of showing off, arrogance, or despair, while imagining that he is engaged in worship. This is one of the great calamities for the one who acts without knowledge.

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:

> نوم على علم خير من صلاة على جهل  
>
> “Sleeping with knowledge is better than praying in ignorance.”

Its meaning is that worship without knowledge does not possess complete value and may even become corrupted.

The second reason is that knowledge nurtures fear of Allah. Allah says:

> إِنَّمَا يَخْشَى اللَّهَ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ الْعُلَمَاءُ  
>
> “Only those among His servants who possess knowledge truly fear Allah.” (Qur’an, Fatir: 28)

A person who knows Allah properly will fear Him properly. He will magnify Him, preserve His commands, and avoid His prohibitions. From knowledge comes obedience, and through knowledge a person is restrained from sin.

For this reason, it is said that a servant has no purpose in worship except two things: performing acts of obedience and avoiding acts of disobedience. And both of these can only be achieved through knowledge.

Therefore, the one who walks the path of the Hereafter must begin everything with knowledge. Knowledge is the light that illuminates the road, the safeguard against misguidance, and the key to all deeds. Without knowledge, a person may go astray in his worship. With knowledge, he walks upon guidance, so his deeds are sound, his heart is purified, and his path to Allah becomes clear. And Allah knows best.

## The Superiority of Knowledge over Worship

In the passage already mentioned, it is explained that knowledge and worship are two jewels that constitute the purpose of creation, yet knowledge occupies a rank higher than worship. This is because knowledge is the origin, while worship is its fruit. Worship cannot be correct, safe, or valuable in the sight of Allah unless it is founded upon sound knowledge. For this reason, knowledge is given priority, because it is the guide and director of every deed performed by the servant.

> فضل العالم على العابد كفضلي على أدنى رجل من أصحابي  
>
> “The superiority of the scholar over the devout worshipper is like my superiority over the lowest-ranking man among my Companions.”

This hadith shows how lofty the rank of knowledge is. The comparison used is not an ordinary one; it is a comparison between the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and the least-ranked of his Companions. This indicates that the difference in degree between the person of knowledge and the one devoted to worship is extremely great, because the person of knowledge knows the correct path, while the worshipper without knowledge may go astray in his deeds.

It is also said:

“Looking at a scholar is more beloved to me than a year of worship with fasting by day and prayer by night.”

The meaning of this statement is not to belittle worship, but to show that knowledge is the source of goodness, while worship is the result of knowledge. Without knowledge, worship may become futile and may even become a form of misguidance. A person may strive intensely in worship, but because he does not know the correct guidance, his deeds may not be accepted or may even become a cause of destruction.

In the passage, a very beautiful parable is given: knowledge is like a tree, and worship is like its fruit. The tree is the source from which fruit grows. Fruit cannot exist without a tree. Yet a tree without fruit provides no benefit. Therefore, the servant must possess both: knowledge as the foundation, and worship as its outcome. However, since knowledge is the foundation, it is nobler and must come first.

Al-Hasan al-Basri رحمه الله gave a profound piece of advice:

“Seek knowledge in a way that does not ruin worship, and seek worship in a way that does not ruin knowledge.”

This statement shows the balance between knowledge and action. Knowledge must not cause a person to abandon worship, and worship must not cause a person to neglect knowledge. Yet in order of priority, knowledge still must come first, because it is the guide of action.

It is also said in the passage:

> العلم إمام العمل والعمل تابعه  
>
> “Knowledge is the leader of action, and action is its follower.”

Its meaning is that knowledge leads and directs deeds. Deeds follow wherever knowledge takes them. If the knowledge is sound, the deeds will be sound. If the knowledge is corrupted, the deeds will also be corrupted. For this reason, a person who acts without knowledge often causes more corruption than reform.

This is reinforced by the saying of the Prophet ﷺ:

> نوم على علم خير من صلاة على جهل  
>
> “Sleeping with knowledge is better than praying in ignorance.”

Its meaning is that a person who possesses knowledge, even if he is not performing worship at that moment, is still in a better condition than one who worships without knowledge. This is because worship without knowledge carries a great risk of error, of being inconsistent with proper guidance, or even of becoming a cause of arrogance and showing off.

Knowledge also has another virtue: it produces fear of Allah. Allah ﷻ says:

> إِنَّمَا يَخْشَى اللَّهَ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ الْعُلَمَاءُ  
>
> “Only those among His servants who possess knowledge truly fear Allah.” (Qur’an, Fatir: 28)

A person of knowledge knows Allah properly, knows His greatness, and understands the consequence of sin and the reward of obedience. Therefore, he fears Allah more, is more careful in his deeds, and is more sincere in his worship.

Thus, the superiority of knowledge over worship does not mean that worship is unimportant. Rather, it means that knowledge is the foundation of worship. Worship without knowledge is like a building without a foundation: it easily collapses and is unstable. Sound knowledge, on the other hand, gives rise to sound worship, a humble heart, and deeds accepted by Allah.

For this reason, a servant who wants to be saved must begin his journey with knowledge, then put that knowledge into practice through worship. Through knowledge, he learns the road. Through worship, he walks that road. And through both together, he reaches the pleasure of Allah. And Allah knows best.

## The Knowledge That Is Obligatory to Learn

After the virtue of knowledge and its higher status than worship have been explained, an important question arises: what kind of knowledge is obligatory for every Muslim to learn? In the passage, the saying of the Prophet ﷺ is mentioned:

> طلب العلم فريضة على كل مسلم  
>
> “Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim.”

However, the scholars explain that not every type of knowledge is obligatory for every individual. The knowledge that is obligatory is the knowledge that a servant needs in order to correct his creed, purify his heart, and perform his worship properly. The text states that the knowledge that is individually obligatory (*fard ‘ayn*) is divided into three parts: the knowledge of tawhid, the knowledge of the heart, and the knowledge of the Sharia.

The first is the knowledge of tawhid, meaning the knowledge related to knowing Allah. What is obligatory to learn from it is the amount that allows a person to possess sound belief. He must know that he has a Lord who is All-Knowing, All-Powerful, All-Willing, Ever-Living, All-Hearing, All-Seeing, and One who speaks. He must believe that Allah is One, has no partner, possesses the attributes of perfection, and is free from every deficiency. He must also know that Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is the true servant and Messenger of Allah, and believe in all the matters of the Hereafter brought by him. This is the foundation of creed that every Muslim must know, because without sound creed, all deeds have no value.

The second is the knowledge of the actions of the heart, referred to in the passage as *‘ilm al-sirr*. This knowledge concerns the inward states of the servant, such as sincerity, reliance upon Allah, patience, contentment, gratitude, repentance, and fear of Allah. A Muslim must know these matters because they are obligations of the heart commanded by Allah in the Qur’an. Allah says:

> وَعَلَى اللَّهِ فَتَوَكَّلُوا إِن كُنتُم مُّؤْمِنِينَ  
>
> “And upon Allah let your trust be placed, if you are truly believers.” (Qur’an, Al-Ma’idah: 23)

Allah also says:

> وَاشْكُرُوا لِلَّهِ إِن كُنتُمْ إِيَّاهُ تَعْبُدُونَ  
>
> “And be grateful to Allah if it is Him alone that you worship.” (Qur’an, Al-Baqarah: 172)

And His saying:

> وَمَا صَبْرُكَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ  
>
> “And your patience is only by the help of Allah.” (Qur’an, An-Nahl: 127)

These verses show that the actions of the heart are obligations that must be learned and acted upon. A person must not be preoccupied only with prayer, fasting, and outward acts of worship while his heart is filled with showing off, arrogance, envy, or despair. Such a condition may corrupt all his outward deeds without him even realizing it.

The third is the knowledge of the Sharia, meaning the knowledge of the rulings related to acts of worship and dealings that the servant is required to perform. Each person is obligated to learn the rulings directly related to the duties binding upon him. For example, one must know the manner of wudu, prayer, and fasting, because these are daily obligations. But he is not required to learn the rulings of Hajj if he is not yet able, nor the rulings of zakat if he does not possess wealth reaching the nisab, nor the rulings of trade if he is not engaged in commerce. In other words, the knowledge of Sharia that is individually obligatory is that which directly concerns the duties a person is carrying out or is about to carry out.

As for delving deeply into wider branches of knowledge, such as refuting deviant sects, mastering all branches of fiqh, or exploring the detailed discussions of theology, then this belongs to *fard kifayah*, not *fard ‘ayn*. It is sufficient that some members of the Muslim community undertake it; if some do so, the obligation falls from the rest.

Thus, a Muslim is not required to master every branch of religious knowledge in depth, but he is obligated to know the knowledge that saves his creed, rectifies his heart, and guides his worship. This is the level of knowledge that every servant must learn.

If a person learns sound tawhid, understands the obligatory inward actions of the heart, and knows the rulings of the acts of worship required of him, then he has fulfilled the obligation of knowledge placed upon him. After that, he must act upon that knowledge in his daily life. With sound knowledge and sincere action, he becomes a servant walking toward Allah in the light of guidance, not in the darkness of ignorance. And Allah knows best.

## The Danger of Seeking Knowledge for Worldly Intentions

After explaining the virtue of knowledge and the obligation of seeking it, the passage also gives a severe warning about the danger of seeking knowledge with the wrong intention, especially a worldly intention. Knowledge in its original nature is a light that leads the servant to Allah. But if the intention is corrupted, that very knowledge may become a cause of destruction.

The passage states that the path of knowledge is difficult, dangerous, and full of risk. Many people turn away from it and go astray, and many others travel it but fall and are disgraced. This is because knowledge is not merely a matter of memorization or information, but a great trust and a heavy responsibility. Whoever pursues it with a sound intention will be safe. But whoever pursues it with a corrupted intention will be ruined.

For that reason, the passage gives a stern warning:

> فمن طلب العلم ليصرف به وجوه الناس إليه ويجالس به الأمراء ويباهي به النظراء ويتصيد به الحطام فتجارته بائرة وصفقته خاسرة  
>
> “Whoever seeks knowledge in order to turn people’s faces toward himself, to sit with rulers, to boast before his peers, and to hunt worldly possessions by means of it, then his trade is ruined and his transaction is a loss.”

This statement explains several corrupt intentions in seeking knowledge, among them:

First, seeking popularity and status in the eyes of people. A person studies so that he may be seen as knowledgeable, respected, praised, and referred to. Knowledge is used as a tool for gaining fame, not for drawing near to Allah.

Second, seeking nearness to rulers and people of influence. He pursues knowledge so that he may sit in the gatherings of officials, obtain rank, or secure a certain position. Knowledge is turned into a ladder toward power and worldly status.

Third, boasting and competing with fellow seekers of knowledge. He learns so that he may appear smarter, higher, or superior to others. His purpose is not to seek truth, but to win debates and display his own excellence.

Fourth, seeking worldly wealth. Knowledge becomes a means of acquiring material gain rather than a means of worship and service to Allah.

A person who holds such intentions is described in the passage as one whose business is a loss. He may have struggled to study, spent much time and energy, but the outcome of all that effort is not the pleasure of Allah but His anger.

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:

> من طلب العلم ليفاخر به العلماء أو ليماري به السفهاء أو ليصرف به وجوه الناس إليه أدخله الله النار  
>
> “Whoever seeks knowledge in order to boast before the scholars, to argue with the foolish, or to turn people’s faces toward himself, Allah will admit him into the Fire.”

This hadith shows that knowledge, which should have been a cause of salvation, may instead become a cause for entering Hell if the intention is corrupted. This is because knowledge is itself an act of worship, and no act of worship is accepted without sincerity.

Abu Yazid al-Bustami رحمه الله said:

“I struggled against myself for thirty years, and I found nothing more difficult for me than knowledge and its dangers.”

This statement shows that the danger of knowledge does not lie in knowledge itself, but in the intention and inner state of the seeker. Knowledge may produce arrogance, showing off, and love of the world if it is not accompanied by sincerity.

The passage also warns against being deceived by Satan. Satan may whisper:

“If knowledge is so dangerous, then leaving it is safer.”

But this is an extremely subtle deception. For abandoning knowledge leads to even greater ruin. It is mentioned in the passage that during the Isra’ and Mi‘raj, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ saw that most of the people of Hell were the ignorant. The meaning is that many people are destroyed because of their ignorance, since they do not know how to worship correctly.

A person without knowledge is unable to perform worship properly. Even if he were to worship like the angels, without knowledge he would still be among the losers. This is because worship without knowledge may go in the wrong direction, fail to conform to the Prophetic way, or even become a cause of arrogance and deviation.

Therefore, the correct path is not to abandon knowledge, but to correct one’s intention in seeking it. Knowledge must be sought with the intention of seeking the pleasure of Allah, reforming oneself, and aiding His religion.

At the end of the passage, it is stated that if a person learns sound tawhid, understands the actions of the heart, and knows the acts of worship obligatory upon him, then acts upon this knowledge, he is among those servants who walk toward Allah with knowledge and deeds. He becomes a servant of knowledge and action, walking upon guidance rather than in the darkness of ignorance.

But if his knowledge is only for worldly gain, position, praise, and wealth, then he is in grave danger. Knowledge, which should have been light, is transformed into a cause of ruin. Therefore, every seeker of knowledge must constantly examine his intention, purify his heart, and remember that the primary purpose of knowledge is to draw near to Allah, not to the world.

Thus, the greatest danger in seeking knowledge does not lie in the hardship of learning, but in the corruption of intention. Whoever’s intention is sound will be saved by his knowledge. And whoever’s intention is corrupted, his knowledge itself will become the cause of his destruction. And Allah knows best.

## The Call to Repentance

After the discussion of knowledge, the passage turns to a matter of immense importance in the journey of a servant, namely repentance. Repentance is described as the first gate to all stations of goodness. A servant will not reach Allah safely unless he purifies himself from sins through sincere repentance.

The passage states the call:

> أيها الناس توبوا إلى الله جميعا
>
> “O people, repent to Allah, all of you.”

This call is in harmony with the word of Allah:

> وَتُوبُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا أَيُّهَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ
>
> “And repent to Allah, all of you, O believers, so that you may succeed.” (Qur’an, An-Nur: 31)

This verse shows that repentance is not only a command for those who have many sins, but for all believers. Even the righteous are still commanded to repent, because human beings are never free from shortcomings, heedlessness, and sins, whether they realize them or not.

Allah also says:

> إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ التَّوَّابِينَ وَيُحِبُّ الْمُتَطَهِّرِينَ
>
> “Indeed, Allah loves those who constantly repent and loves those who purify themselves.” (Qur’an, Al-Baqarah: 222)

This verse shows the nobility of repentance. Repentance is not merely an eraser of sins, but also a cause for a person to attain the love of Allah. Not everyone attains His love, but those who repent are among those whom He loves.

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:

> إنه ليغان على قلبي وإني لأستغفر الله في اليوم سبعين مرة
>
> “Indeed, a veil sometimes comes over my heart, and I seek Allah’s forgiveness seventy times in a day.”

If the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, who is protected from sin, would still seek forgiveness every day, then ordinary human beings are surely even more in need of repentance and seeking forgiveness.

The passage mentions that a person who wishes to repent must constantly practice *muhasabah*, meaning self-examination, every day. He must reflect on what he has done throughout his day. If he finds good in it, he should thank Allah. If he finds sin in it, he should regret it, blame himself, and immediately repent.

It is further emphasized that blaming oneself must be done seriously, not with a light heart or with laughter. A servant must rebuke himself with a sorrowful heart, full of regret, and feeling humbled before Allah. If he does this sincerely, then Allah will replace his sadness with joy, his humiliation with honor, his darkness with light, and his veiling with the opening of the path toward Him.

The passage also gives a very strong parable. It is mentioned that every sin creates darkness in the heart. Sin is likened to fire, while the darkness of the heart is like its smoke. If a person were to light a fire inside a house for years, the walls of that house would surely become blackened. Likewise, the human heart. Every sin leaves a dark mark upon the heart. If sins continue without repentance, the heart becomes darker and harder.

A darkened heart can only be cleansed through repentance. If repentance is immediate, the stain is easily removed. But if sins continue to be repeated without repentance, the stain will settle and remain until the heart becomes hard and unable to receive the truth.

For that reason, it is said that repentance is the beginning of every spiritual station. No spiritual station after repentance can be accepted without repentance. Repentance is likened to washing a cooking pot blackened by soot. If it is washed immediately, the dirt is easy to remove. But if it is left for a long time and continues to be used for cooking, the dirt will become attached and hard to remove.

Repentance is also described as a gift from Allah. Not everyone is granted success in repentance. It may be that a servant who is poor, weak, and unknown is granted repentance by Allah, while a wealthy and respected person is not granted it. Therefore, if a person is granted the ability to repent, this is a sign that Allah loves him.

It is also mentioned:

> إنما يغتبط بالشيء من يعرف قدره
>
> “Only the one who knows the value of something will rejoice in it.”

A person who understands the value of repentance will rejoice when he is given the opportunity to repent. But the one who does not understand its value will treat it lightly.

Allah says:

> وَمَن لَّمْ يَتُبْ فَأُولَئِكَ هُمُ الظَّالِمُونَ
>
> “And whoever does not repent—then it is they who are the wrongdoers.” (Qur’an, Al-Hujurat: 11)

This verse shows that the one who does not repent is counted among those who wrong themselves.

It is also mentioned that when a servant repents, his house in Paradise rejoices, the heavens and the earth rejoice, and even the Messenger of Allah ﷺ rejoices in his repentance. This shows how great the rank of repentance is in the sight of Allah.

On the other hand, sin is described as something extremely ugly. It is astonishing that a servant who knows the goodness of his Lord would still dare to disobey Him. The one who sins has, in reality, not truly known Allah. If he truly knew Allah’s greatness, His nearness, and His watchfulness, he would be ashamed to commit sin.

Sin does not only ruin one’s Hereafter, but also brings harmful effects in this world and in the heart. Sin darkens the heart, obstructs a person from goodness, and distances the servant from his Lord. For this reason, repentance becomes the only path to cleanse the heart and return to Allah.

Thus, the call to repentance is not merely advice; it is a call to salvation. Repentance is the first door to all goodness, a source of light for the heart, and a cause of attaining the love of Allah. Whoever repents will succeed. Whoever delays repentance is in great danger. And Allah knows best.

## The Importance of Following the Prophet ﷺ

After the call to repentance, the passage affirms that a servant’s salvation will not be complete except by following the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. Repentance cleanses the heart from sin, but the path toward nearness to Allah can only be traveled by following the guidance of the Prophet ﷺ. Therefore, *ittiba‘*—following the Prophet—becomes the foundation for the acceptance of deeds and the principal cause of a servant’s honor in the sight of Allah.

It is stated in the passage:

> ولا تحصل لك الرفعة عند الله تعالى إلا بمتابعة النبي صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم
>
> “You will not attain high rank with Allah except by following the Prophet ﷺ.”

This expression shows that all honor and rank in the sight of Allah depend on the extent to which a person follows the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. Abundant deeds, strenuous worship, and great effort will have no value if they do not conform to his guidance.

The passage explains that following the Prophet ﷺ is divided into two parts: outward and inward.

First, outward following means carrying out the Sharia that he brought, such as prayer, fasting, zakat, Hajj, jihad, and all other outward acts of worship. This is the form of *ittiba‘* that is visible in action.

Second, inward following means bringing humility, presence, and awareness of the heart into worship. For example, maintaining humility in prayer and reflection when reciting the Qur’an. If a person performs outward acts of worship but does not feel the presence of the heart, this is a sign of an inward disease such as arrogance, showing off, or other diseases of the heart.

Allah says:

> سَأَصْرِفُ عَنْ آيَاتِيَ الَّذِينَ يَتَكَبَّرُونَ فِي الْأَرْضِ بِغَيْرِ الْحَقِّ
>
> “I will turn away from My signs those who are arrogant upon the earth without right.” (Qur’an, Al-A‘raf: 146)

This verse shows that arrogance can prevent a person from understanding the signs of Allah. A proud heart will not taste the sweetness of worship, just as a person suffering from fever tastes sweet food as bitter.

For that reason, the passage emphasizes one important principle:

> المعصية مع الذل والافتقار خير من الطاعة مع العجب والاستكبار
>
> “A sin accompanied by humility and neediness before Allah is better than an act of obedience accompanied by self-admiration and arrogance.”

Its meaning is that a sin which makes a person regretful, humbled before Allah, and return to Him through repentance is still better than worship that makes a person arrogant, self-satisfied, and disdainful of others. Arrogance in worship can destroy all deeds, while humility in sin can become a path toward repentance and salvation.

The passage also stresses that following the Prophet is a sign of one’s connection to him, whereas abandoning him is a sign that this connection has been severed.

Allah says, quoting Prophet Ibrahim عليه السلام:

> فَمَن تَبِعَنِي فَإِنَّهُ مِنِّي
>
> “Whoever follows me is indeed from me.” (Qur’an, Ibrahim: 36)

Its meaning is that whoever follows the prophets is counted among them, even if he is not of their lineage.

Conversely, Allah said to Prophet Nuh عليه السلام concerning his son:

> إِنَّهُ لَيْسَ مِنْ أَهْلِكَ إِنَّهُ عَمَلٌ غَيْرُ صَالِحٍ
>
> “Indeed, he is not of your family; indeed, his conduct is unrighteous.” (Qur’an, Hud: 46)

This verse shows that the true bond is not the bond of blood, but the bond of faith and obedience. A person may be cut off from the family of the prophets because of his deeds, and another may be near to them because he follows their path.

The passage gives a very beautiful example in the story of Salman al-Farisi رضي الله عنه. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:

> سلمان منا أهل البيت
>
> “Salman is from us, from the People of the Household.”

Yet Salman was from Persia, not from the Arabs, much less from the Prophet’s own family. But because of his faith and obedience, he was included in the family of the Prophet in a spiritual sense. This shows that rank before Allah is determined by following, not by lineage.

The passage also states that all goodness is gathered within one house, and the key to that house is following the Prophet ﷺ. This means that whoever wishes to attain all goodness must open that door with the key of *ittiba‘* to the Messenger of Allah.

Following the Prophet means not only in acts of worship, but also in daily life, such as being content with one’s provision, being detached from worldly excess, reducing what is of no benefit, and guarding one’s speech and actions.

Allah says:

> قُلْ إِن كُنتُمْ تُحِبُّونَ اللَّهَ فَاتَّبِعُونِي يُحْبِبْكُمُ اللَّهُ
>
> “Say: If you love Allah, then follow me, and Allah will love you.” (Qur’an, Ali ‘Imran: 31)

This verse shows that following the Prophet is the proof of love for Allah and the cause by which a person gains the love of Allah. Without *ittiba‘*, the claim of loving Allah has no meaning.

The passage also reminds us that one of the conditions for attaining goodness is not oppressing other people, whether with regard to their wealth, their honor, or their lineage. Many people are obstructed from the journey to Allah because of sins committed against other human beings. They are like debtors who cannot be freed because of the claims held against them by others.

Sins against people are extremely grave, because on the Day of Judgment, those who were wronged will demand their rights. If a person comes with many good deeds, but many people claim against him because of his ظلم, his good deeds will be given to them until he becomes bankrupt.

For this reason, following the Prophet ﷺ is not only in acts of worship, but also in character, justice, and one’s dealings with others.

Thus, the importance of following the Prophet ﷺ lies not only in the outward forms of worship, but also in the condition of the heart, one’s character, and every aspect of life. *Ittiba‘* is the path to the love of Allah, the cause of honor in His sight, and the sign of one’s connection to the prophets and the righteous.

Whoever follows the Prophet ﷺ is counted among his people. Whoever turns away from him is cut off from the path of salvation. And Allah knows best.

## The Virtue of Repentance

In the passage already mentioned, repentance is described as something immensely noble and exalted, and indeed as the beginning of all spiritual stations in the journey toward Allah. A servant will not attain higher goodness until he purifies himself from sins through sincere repentance.

It is stated in the passage:

> التوبة أول المقامات ولا يقبل ما بعدها إلا بها
>
> “Repentance is the first of all spiritual stations, and nothing that comes after it is accepted except through it.”

The meaning of this statement is that all deeds and spiritual ranks have no value unless they are preceded by repentance. Just as a person who wishes to build a house must first clear the land of dirt and rubble, so too must the human heart be cleansed. As long as the heart remains filled with the stains of sin, acts of goodness cannot shine perfectly.

Repentance holds such a high rank because it is a cause for a person to attain the love of Allah. Allah says:

> إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ التَّوَّابِينَ وَيُحِبُّ الْمُتَطَهِّرِينَ
>
> “Indeed, Allah loves those who constantly repent and loves those who purify themselves.” (Qur’an, Al-Baqarah: 222)

This verse shows that repentance is not merely an eraser of sins, but also a path to the love of Allah. Not everyone gains His love, but those who repent are among those whom He loves. Therefore, the person who is granted success in repentance has, in fact, been given a very great blessing.

The passage also states that repentance is a gift from Allah, not merely a human effort. There are people who appear noble, respected, and knowledgeable, yet they are not granted the success to repent. On the other hand, there may be a person who is poor, weak, and unknown, yet Allah grants him repentance and return to Him. Therefore, if a person is given the ability to repent, this is a sign that Allah intends good for him.

It is also stated that the one who knows the value of repentance will rejoice greatly in it:

> إنما يغتبط بالشيء من يعرف قدره
>
> “Only the one who knows the value of something will rejoice in it.”

That is, the person who understands how great repentance is will be happy when he is given the opportunity to return to Allah. He will not feel burdened by it, nor forced into it, but rather grateful that he has still been granted time to reform himself.

Conversely, the person who does not repent is described as a wrongdoer. Allah says:

> وَمَن لَّمْ يَتُبْ فَأُولَئِكَ هُمُ الظَّالِمُونَ
>
> “And whoever does not repent—then it is they who are the wrongdoers.” (Qur’an, Al-Hujurat: 11)

This shows that abandoning repentance is not a light matter; rather, it is a form of wrongdoing against oneself. The person who persists in sin without repentance is, in reality, casting himself into destruction.

The passage also explains that repentance brings joy not only to the servant himself, but even to the unseen world. It is said that when a servant repents, his house in Paradise rejoices, the heavens and the earth rejoice, and even the Messenger of Allah ﷺ rejoices in his repentance. This shows how great the value of repentance is in the sight of Allah. Repentance is not merely a personal change, but a great event welcomed with joy in the unseen world.

The passage also offers a very clear parable regarding repentance. The human heart is likened to a vessel or a cooking pot. If that pot is touched by soot for only a short time and then cleaned immediately, its dirt is easily removed. But if the pot continues to be used for cooking without being cleaned, its soot will cling and become difficult to remove.

Likewise is the human heart. If a person commits a sin and then immediately repents, the sin is easily erased. But if he continues to repeat the sin without repentance, the stain remains and hardens in the heart. Eventually, the heart becomes dark, hard, and unable to receive the truth.

Therefore, repentance must be done immediately, before the heart becomes hardened. As long as life remains, the door of repentance remains open. The passage mentions that as long as a person is still alive, he still has hope to repent.

It is also said:

> من تاب ظفر
>
> “Whoever repents will triumph.”

That is, true victory is not found in wealth, rank, or power, but in the ability to return to Allah and abandon sin.

The passage also describes how terrible the state of the person who continues in sin really is. It is truly pitiable that a servant knows the goodness of his Lord, yet still dares to disobey Him. Such a person has not truly known Allah. If he truly knew Allah’s greatness, His nearness, and His watchfulness, he would be ashamed to sin.

Thus, repentance is not merely an obligation, but also a form of awareness and knowledge of Allah. The more a person knows his Lord, the more ashamed he becomes to disobey Him, and the more he is driven to repent.

Therefore, the virtue of repentance lies in many things: it is the beginning of all stations of goodness, it is a cause of attaining the love of Allah, it cleanses the heart from the darkness of sin, it brings joy in the heavens and the earth, and it is a sign that Allah intends good for His servant.

For this reason, repentance is the door of salvation. Whoever opens it will be saved. Whoever shuts it upon himself will fall into destruction. A servant should therefore continually renew his repentance, seek forgiveness from Allah, and never despair of His mercy. And Allah knows best.

## The Danger of Sin

In the passage that has been mentioned, the danger of sin and its impact upon the heart of a servant are explained very clearly. Sin is not merely a violation of the rulings of Sharia; it has a very deep effect on a person’s inward state, and can even become a cause of destruction in this world and the Hereafter.

The passage mentions that every sin committed by a servant leaves darkness in the heart. The parable used is very clear:

> إذا وقع من العبد ذنب وقع معه ظلمة، فمثال المعصية كالنار، والظلمة دخانها
>
> “If a servant commits a sin, darkness falls with it. The example of sin is like fire, and the darkness is its smoke.”

That is, sin never stands alone. Every sin inevitably leaves a mark, just as fire always produces smoke. That smoke blackens a room, and likewise sin blackens the heart. The more sins there are, the thicker the darkness that covers the heart.

The passage gives a very tangible example: if a person lights a fire inside a house for many years, the walls of that house will become black. Likewise with the human heart. If sins are committed continuously without repentance, the heart becomes darker, harder, and farther from the light of faith.

A dark heart does not easily receive the truth. It becomes heavy in worship, lazy in obedience, and quick to slip into disobedience. This is one of the greatest dangers of sin: not only punishment in the Hereafter, but the corruption of the heart in this world.

Sin also produces three harmful things in the heart, as mentioned in the passage: darkness, humiliation, and veiling from Allah. That is, sin darkens the heart, humiliates the soul, and creates a barrier between the servant and his Lord. The more sins there are, the thicker that veil becomes, until the servant no longer tastes the sweetness of worship and no longer feels sensitive to the truth.

The passage also explains that repentance is the only way to remove the traces of sin. If a person repents quickly, that darkness disappears. But if he delays repentance and continues repeating the sin, the darkness remains and becomes difficult to remove.

The parable used is powerful. The heart is likened to a vessel or a pot. If the pot is touched by soot only briefly and then cleaned immediately, it becomes clean again. But if the pot continues to be used without being cleaned, the soot will cling strongly and may even damage the vessel itself.

Likewise with the human heart. A small sin, if immediately erased through repentance, will not ruin the heart. But sins that are repeated without repentance will accumulate, harden, and eventually corrupt the heart completely.

The passage also warns against the ugliness of daring to disobey Allah. It states that it is a miserable condition when a servant knows the goodness of his Lord yet still dares to violate His command. The one who sins has not truly known Allah.

It is stated that the person who knows Allah’s greatness will not dare to disobey Him; the one who knows that Allah always sees him will not violate His command; and the one who knows the consequence of sin in this world and the Hereafter will be ashamed before his Lord. In other words, sin is not only the result of weakness, but also the result of insufficient knowledge of Allah. The more a person knows his Lord, the more afraid he becomes to commit sin.

The passage also explains that sin has far-reaching effects: it corrupts the heart, obstructs a person from goodness, distances the servant from Allah, and brings humiliation in both this world and the Hereafter. A person may know that his lower self is calling him to destruction, yet still follow it. He may know that his heart is calling him to goodness, yet still reject it. He may know Allah’s greatness, yet still dare to oppose His commands. This is a sign of the servant’s destruction.

The danger of sin also lies in its lasting effect. Sin is not merely a passing mistake; it leaves traces that can accumulate and harden. If left unchecked, it may seal the heart completely, until the person no longer feels regret, no longer fears Allah, and no longer cares about goodness.

For this reason, sin must be immediately followed by repentance. As long as a person still feels regret and wants to return to Allah, his heart is still alive. But if sins are committed without regret, that is a sign that the heart has begun to die.

Thus, the danger of sin lies not only in the punishment waiting in the Hereafter, but also in the corruption of the heart in this world. Sin darkens the heart, humiliates the soul, and veils the servant from his Lord. Therefore, a servant must always be cautious of sin, no matter how small, and quickly return to Allah in repentance before his heart becomes dark and hard. And Allah knows best.

### Conclusion

From the entire discussion that has been presented, it becomes clear that a servant’s journey toward salvation cannot be separated from three foundational matters: knowledge, repentance, and following the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. These three are pillars that are interconnected and cannot be separated. Knowledge guides the way, repentance cleanses the heart, and following the Prophet perfects one’s deeds.

Knowledge is the first ascent that must be crossed, because without knowledge, a servant will not know his Lord, will not know his obligations, and will not be able to distinguish between obedience and disobedience. Knowledge is the light that illuminates the path to Allah, and it serves as the leader of all deeds. Without knowledge, worship may become fruitless and may even become a cause of destruction if it is performed without correct guidance or with corrupt intention.

However, knowledge also carries a great danger if it is not accompanied by sincerity. Knowledge sought for the sake of the world, popularity, or status will instead become a cause of ruin for its possessor. Therefore, the seeker of knowledge must always guard his intention and make knowledge a path to the pleasure of Allah, not a means to worldly gain.

After knowledge, a servant must continually renew his repentance. Repentance is the first gate of all spiritual stations, and no deed is accepted without it. Sins that are repeatedly committed will darken the heart, humiliate the soul, and veil the servant from his Lord. Only through repentance does the heart become pure, deeds become illuminated, and the path to Allah open once again.

Repentance is also a sign of Allah’s love for His servant. Whoever is granted the success to repent has been granted a tremendous blessing. For this reason, a servant must never delay repentance, never despair, and never feel secure from sin. As long as life remains, the door of repentance remains open.

After this, a servant’s salvation depends on the degree to which he follows the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. *Ittiba‘* of the Prophet is the key to all goodness and the path to the love of Allah. No matter how many deeds a person performs, they will have no value if they do not conform to his guidance. Following the Prophet is not only in outward acts of worship, but also in the state of the heart, in one’s character, and in all aspects of life.

The one who follows the Prophet belongs to his people, even if he is not from his lineage. Conversely, the one who turns away from him is cut off from the path of salvation, even if he is related by blood to righteous people. Honor in the sight of Allah is not determined by lineage, but by faith and following.

All of this shows that the path to Allah is a clear path, but it requires seriousness and sincerity. It begins with sound knowledge, continues with sincere repentance, and is completed by following the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. Whoever takes this path truthfully and sincerely will reach the pleasure of Allah and salvation in the Hereafter.

May Allah grant us beneficial knowledge, sincere repentance, and the ability to follow His Messenger in every circumstance. And may He make us among His servants who are safe in this world and the Hereafter. And Allah knows best.
