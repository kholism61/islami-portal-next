## Introduction

In its effort to revive the spiritual atmosphere of Ramadan, Al-Jami' al-Azhar has organized a series of scholarly and devotional activities inside the historic mosque. This program forms part of the Ramadan da'wah plan carried out under the direction of the Grand Imam, Dr. Ahmad Al-Tayyib, and under the supervision of the Deputy of Al-Azhar, Dr. Muhammad Al-Duwaini. Among its central activities is *Dars Tarawih*, a scholarly lesson delivered between units of the nightly tarawih prayer by senior scholars and leaders of Al-Azhar. The program demonstrates that Ramadan is not only a time of ritual devotion, but also a season of intellectual and moral formation.

## Fasting as a School of Character and Integrity

In one of the lessons, Dr. Muhammad Al-Duwaini explained that fasting was prescribed to educate the soul and shape conduct, not only in a servant's relationship with Allah, but also in his dealings with other people. Fasting trains the Muslim to abandon even what is normally lawful for the sake of obedience to Allah. This training plants a deep awareness of divine watchfulness.

For that reason, after mentioning the verses of fasting, the Qur'an warns against consuming the wealth of others unjustly. The message is clear: the one who can restrain hunger and thirst for Allah's sake should be even more capable of restraining himself from fraud, deception, price manipulation, and every form of injustice in transactions. In this way, fasting becomes a school of honesty and public trust.

## Lessons from the Battle of Badr

In another lecture on the lessons of the Battle of Badr, Dr. Ahmad Ma'bad Abd al-Karim emphasized that true victory comes from Allah alone. Badr teaches that numerical weakness and material shortage do not prevent divine support from arriving. The early Muslims, though few in number, were granted victory because of the strength of their faith and supplication.

From this event, three major lessons may be drawn: victory belongs to Allah, outward weakness does not prevent success when faith is strong, and seeking Allah's help with certainty is one of the keys to triumph. In the context of Ramadan, these lessons nourish hope and complete reliance upon Allah in the face of life's challenges.

## Humility as a Foundation of Social Life

Dr. Salamah Dawud stressed the importance of humility in the life of a Muslim. Human beings naturally dislike arrogance, and the one who speaks truth in a condescending tone often produces resistance even when the content is correct.

Islam teaches gentleness and humility. Allah commanded the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ to lower his wing to the believers. True humility does not mean degrading oneself, but placing oneself properly without imagining superiority over others. In the spiritually charged atmosphere of Ramadan, humility protects righteous deeds from being corrupted by pride.

## Reliance on Allah and Human Effort

In another lesson, Dr. Ahmad Hammam explained that reliance upon Allah must go hand in hand with real effort. Reliance without action is passivity, and effort without reliance becomes dependence on human ability alone. Islam teaches balance between the two.

The story of Maryam, who was still commanded to shake the date-palm despite her weakness, shows that human beings must take the means while entrusting the result to Allah. The hadith about the bird that leaves hungry in the morning and returns full in the evening likewise shows that true tawakkul is not idle waiting. This principle forms a personality that is productive yet inwardly dependent on Allah.

### Conclusion

The Ramadan lectures at Al-Jami' al-Azhar show that Ramadan is a school for character and civilization. Fasting builds integrity and honesty, the lessons of Badr nourish certainty in Allah's help, humility refines social conduct, and tawakkul teaches the balance between faith and action.

All of these meanings are joined in one purpose: to form a Muslim who is faithful, ethical, and beneficial to society. In that sense, Ramadan is not merely an annual ritual. It is a season of personal transformation toward a life that is straighter, more just, and more blessed.
