### Ramadan Notes: Honoring One's Parents, a Path to Paradise and a Source of Blessing

On the fifth night of Ramadan 1447 H, after the tarawih prayer, Al-Azhar Mosque held a gathering on “The Rights of Parents in Islam.” The discussion emphasized that disobedience to parents is among the sins whose punishment may be hastened in this world before the Hereafter, while dutifulness to them is among the causes of blessing in a person's life.

The speakers explained that in the noble days of Ramadan, Muslims are more in need of drawing near to Allah through obligatory duties before voluntary acts. In light of the hadith qudsi, “My servant does not draw near to Me with anything more beloved to Me than what I have made obligatory upon him,” honoring parents stands among the greatest obligations that must be prioritized. Night prayer, charity, and other supererogatory acts lose their value when a person neglects or wrongs his parents.

The Qur'an repeatedly joins the worship of Allah with kindness to parents. This pairing shows their exalted rank in Islam and makes clear that dutifulness to them is not merely a noble virtue, but a binding religious obligation.

The Prophet ﷺ said, “May he be humbled, may he be humbled, may he be humbled - the one who finds one or both of his parents in old age and still does not enter Paradise.” This hadith shows that parents can become one of the gates to Paradise, or, Allah protect us, a reason for being deprived of it. Even a light word of annoyance is forbidden in the Qur'an.

Allah also joins gratitude to Him with gratitude to one's parents. This teaches that gratitude to Allah is not complete without gratitude to those through whom one was raised, cared for, and guided. Serving them is therefore part of complete servitude to Allah.

Among the rights of parents are humility before them, respect in speech and action, financial support especially in old age, seeking their permission and prayer when travelling, and avoiding every word or deed that hurts them. The supplication of parents for their child is answered, and a child's life is deeply affected by the pleasure or pain he causes them.

Honoring parents does not end with their death. Part of dutifulness after their passing includes carrying out their good wishes, giving charity on their behalf, performing Hajj or 'umrah for them when possible, honoring their friends, and maintaining ties with the people they loved.

In this way, dutifulness to parents becomes a strong foundation for a righteous Muslim family and a stable society. It is a cause of blessing in life, health, wealth, and offspring, and a path to the pleasure of Allah and success in this world and the next.
