## Introduction

The Qur’an, as the sacred scripture of the Muslim ummah, contains not only laws and guidance for life, but also clearly portrays the nobility of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Allah ﷻ Himself praises him, raises his rank, and explains his exalted qualities in many verses. This shows that the position of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ is not only that of the bearer of revelation, but also that of mercy, light, and an example for all humanity.

The Qur’anic verses that speak about him are not merely words of praise, but also affirmations of the function of prophethood, the greatness of his character, and the obligation of the ummah to love, follow, and honor him. Therefore, understanding the portrayal of the Prophet ﷺ in the Qur’an is an important part of faith and love for him.

## A Messenger Full of Compassion for the Ummah

Among the greatest portrayals of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the Qur’an is his compassion and gentleness toward his ummah. Allah ﷻ Himself describes His Messenger with qualities full of love, care, and deep concern for the salvation of humanity. Allah says:

> لَقَدْ جَاءَكُمْ رَسُولٌ مِنْ أَنْفُسِكُمْ عَزِيزٌ عَلَيْهِ مَا عَنِتُّمْ حَرِيصٌ عَلَيْكُمْ بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ رَءُوفٌ رَحِيمٌ
>
> “There has certainly come to you a Messenger from among yourselves. Grievous to him is what you suffer; he is deeply concerned for you, and toward the believers he is most kind and most merciful.” (QS. At-Tawbah: 128)

This verse is one of the clearest verses in describing the character of the Prophet ﷺ. Allah mentions that he is “from among yourselves” (مِنْ أَنْفُسِكُمْ), meaning from the same people, language, and environment as his ummah. This shows that he was not a foreign figure unknown to them, but someone whose honesty, trustworthiness, noble character, and purity of lineage had already been known even before prophethood.

The mufassirun explain that the phrase “from among yourselves” contains several wisdoms. First, so that the ummah could easily understand his language and teachings. Second, so that they would have no doubt about his honesty and trustworthiness, because they had lived with him and witnessed the nobility of his character. Third, so that this closeness of lineage and social relation would become proof that his da‘wah was not driven by worldly ambition, but by a divine trust.

Then Allah describes the Prophet’s feeling toward the suffering of his ummah in His words: عَزِيزٌ عَلَيْهِ مَا عَنِتُّمْ. This means that every hardship, distress, and suffering that afflicted his ummah weighed heavily upon him. The Prophet ﷺ never felt at ease when his ummah was in difficulty, whether worldly hardship or misguidance that would lead to destruction in the Hereafter.

Next, Allah says: حَرِيصٌ عَلَيْكُمْ, meaning that he intensely desired goodness, safety, and guidance for his ummah. There was no prophet more eager for the salvation of his people than Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Throughout his life, he faced rejection, insults, and persecution, yet he never ceased praying for his people to receive guidance.

Even when he was harmed and driven from his homeland, the prayer that came from his lips was not a prayer for destruction, but a prayer for guidance. This reflects the peak of a prophet’s compassion for his people.

Then Allah concludes the verse with two most exalted qualities:

> بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ رَءُوفٌ رَحِيمٌ
>
> “Toward the believers he is most kind and most merciful.”

The word رَءُوفٌ indicates an extremely deep tenderness of heart, while رَحِيمٌ indicates constant and all-encompassing mercy. Some scholars mention that these two names are also among the names of Allah, so Allah’s describing the Prophet ﷺ with them shows how high his rank is in the sight of Allah.

The meaning of the Prophet’s compassion for his ummah is not limited to worldly life, but extends even to the Hereafter. He was always concerned about the salvation of his ummah before Allah. In various narrations, it is mentioned that he wept and prayed for his ummah, asking forgiveness and safety for them.

His compassion was also evident in the way he taught the religion. He did not burden his ummah, did not make worship difficult for them, and always chose the easier path so long as it did not involve sin. This principle is in harmony with the purpose for which he was sent: as a mercy to all worlds.

The same is affirmed in the word of Allah:

> وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ إِلَّا رَحْمَةً لِلْعَالَمِينَ
>
> “And We have not sent you except as a mercy to all the worlds.” (QS. Al-Anbiya’: 107)

This verse shows that the very existence of the Prophet ﷺ is mercy. His life brought mercy, his teachings brought mercy, and even his passing was mercy, because he left behind guidance that saves his ummah.

The ulama explain that his mercy encompasses all creation:

- For the believers, he is mercy in the form of guidance, knowledge, and salvation.

- For the hypocrites, he became the reason their blood and property were protected.

- For the disbelievers, he became the reason punishment was delayed, unlike what befell earlier nations.

The Prophet’s compassion ﷺ was also evident in his patience in facing all forms of harm. For more than twenty years of da‘wah, he endured mockery, boycott, exile, and even attempts on his life. Yet none of this caused him to stop desiring salvation for his people.

All of these qualities show that Prophet Muhammad ﷺ represents the highest peak of human compassion toward fellow human beings. He did not merely teach religion, but also became the living example of how love, care, and gentleness can become the path to salvation.

By understanding this verse, a believer realizes that loving the Prophet ﷺ is not merely an obligation, but also the proper response to his immense compassion for his ummah. For there is no one who desired the salvation of humankind more than the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

## The Great Favor: The Sending of the Messenger

Among the greatest blessings that Allah ﷻ bestowed upon humanity is the sending of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ among them. Allah Himself affirms that his presence was not merely a historical event, but a magnificent divine favor, especially for the believers. Allah says:

> لُقَدْ مَنَّ اللَّهُ عَلَى الْمُؤْمِنِينَ إِذْ بَعَثَ فِيهِمْ رَسُولًا مِنْ أَنْفُسِهِمْ يَتْلُو عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتِهِ وَيُزَكِّيهِمْ وَيُعَلِّمُهُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْحِكْمَةَ وَإِنْ كَانُوا مِنْ قَبْلُ لَفِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ
>
> “Allah has certainly bestowed a great favor upon the believers when He raised among them a Messenger from among themselves, reciting to them His verses, purifying them, and teaching them the Book and wisdom, while before that they were surely in clear error.” (QS. Ali ‘Imran: 164)

This verse begins with the expression لَقَدْ مَنَّ اللَّهُ, which indicates a blessing that is immense and magnificent. The word *mann* in Arabic does not simply mean “to give,” but to give a great and priceless favor. This shows that the sending of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is the greatest blessing Allah gave to humankind.

Allah mentions that this Messenger was sent “from among themselves” (مِنْ أَنْفُسِهِمْ). This contains great wisdom. Since he came from the same people, spoke the same language, and lived in the midst of their society, they had no reason to doubt his honesty, trustworthiness, and the truth of his message. They had known him from his youth as a truthful person, so his prophethood became even stronger proof of the truth of the message he brought.

This verse also explains the three principal tasks of prophethood, which form the essence of the mission of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ:

1. Reciting the verses of Allah

His word: يَتْلُو عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتِهِ. He recited the revelation of Allah to humanity, conveying the truth that came from heaven. This recitation was not merely uttered words, but carried guidance, warning, and glad tidings for humankind.

2. Purifying human souls

His word: وَيُزَكِّيهِمْ. The task of the Messenger ﷺ was not only to deliver revelation, but also to purify human hearts from shirk, ignorance, bad character, and all diseases of the heart. He guided humanity from the darkness into the light of faith and noble character.

2. Purifying human souls

His word: وَيُزَكِّيهِمْ. The task of the Messenger ﷺ was not only to deliver revelation, but also to purify human hearts from shirk, ignorance, bad character, and all diseases of the heart. He guided humanity from the darkness into the light of faith and noble character.

3. Teaching the Book and wisdom

His word: وَيُعَلِّمُهُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْحِكْمَةَ. He taught the Qur’an as the Book of guidance, as well as wisdom, which the ulama understood as the Sunnah or a deep understanding of religion. Thus, he not only conveyed revelation, but also explained it, interpreted it, and practiced it in real life.

This verse closes with the statement:

> وَإِنْ كَانُوا مِنْ قَبْلُ لَفِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ
>
> “While before that they were surely in clear misguidance.”

This describes the condition of society before the coming of the Prophet ﷺ. They lived in the darkness of jahiliyyah, without the guidance of revelation, filled with shirk, hostility, and moral corruption. Thus, the sending of the Prophet ﷺ became a major turning point in human history, from misguidance to the light of faith.

The same meaning is also affirmed in the word of Allah:

> هُوَ الَّذِي بَعَثَ فِي الْأُمِّيِّينَ رَسُولًا مِنْهُمْ يَتْلُو عَلَيْهِمْ آيَاتِهِ وَيُزَكِّيهِمْ وَيُعَلِّمُهُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْحِكْمَةَ وَإِنْ كَانُوا مِنْ قَبْلُ لَفِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ
>
> “He is the One who sent among the unlettered people a Messenger from among themselves, reciting to them His verses, purifying them, and teaching them the Book and wisdom, while before that they were surely in clear misguidance.” (QS. Al-Jumu‘ah: 2)

This verse shows that the Arab people, who previously had no scripture and were not known as a people of knowledge, were transformed into a community that carried civilization and knowledge, all because of the sending of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

Likewise, Allah says:

> كَمَا أَرْسَلْنَا فِيكُمْ رَسُولًا مِنْكُمْ يَتْلُو عَلَيْكُمْ آيَاتِنَا وَيُزَكِّيكُمْ وَيُعَلِّمُكُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْحِكْمَةَ وَيُعَلِّمُكُمْ مَا لَمْ تَكُونُوا تَعْلَمُونَ
>
> “Just as We have sent among you a Messenger from among yourselves, reciting to you Our verses, purifying you, teaching you the Book and wisdom, and teaching you that which you did not know.” (QS. Al-Baqarah: 151)

This verse adds an important dimension, namely that the Prophet ﷺ taught humanity things they did not previously know. This shows that the Islamic civilization, knowledge, character, and system of life brought by him are a great blessing that humanity could never have attained without revelation.

Thus, the sending of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is a blessing encompassing every aspect of life:

- The blessing of faith.

- The blessing of knowledge.

- The blessing of character.

- The blessing of guidance toward salvation.

Without his being sent, humanity would have remained in the darkness of misguidance. For this reason, the Qur’an describes his coming as the greatest favor from Allah to humankind.

## Mercy to All Creation

Among the greatest verses in describing the rank of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is the word of Allah ﷻ affirming that he was sent as a mercy encompassing all creation. Allah says:

> وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ إِلَّا رَحْمَةً لِلْعَالَمِينَ
>
> “And We have not sent you, [O Muhammad], except as a mercy to all the worlds.” (QS. Al-Anbiya: 107)

This verse is the primary foundation showing that the entire message of the Prophet ﷺ is mercy. The word “mercy” in this verse comes in a general form, without restriction, indicating that his mercy encompasses all worlds: humankind, jinn, and even other creatures.

The scholars explain that this verse does not merely mean that the teachings of the Prophet ﷺ bring mercy, but that his very existence itself is mercy. His life was mercy, his da‘wah was mercy, his character was mercy, and even his passing was mercy, because he left behind perfect guidance for his ummah.

The mercy brought by the Prophet ﷺ has different levels according to the condition of people.

Mercy for the believers

For the believers, the Prophet ﷺ is mercy in the form of guidance, faith, knowledge, and salvation in this world and the Hereafter. Through him, human beings come to know Allah, understand His sharia, and find the path to eternal happiness.

His teachings purify the soul, refine character, and elevate people from ignorance to knowledge. By following his Sunnah, a believer attains peace of heart, nobility of character, and salvation in the Hereafter. Mercy for those who do not believe

The mercy of the Prophet ﷺ is not limited only to the believers. Even those who rejected his message still received a share of that mercy.

Earlier nations that denied their prophets were often struck by punishment that destroyed them immediately. But when Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was sent, such punishment was no longer sent generally upon those who denied him. This is a form of mercy that encompasses all humanity.

They were also given the opportunity to repent and return to the truth, rather than being destroyed immediately like the nations before them.

Mercy in character and in sharia

The mercy of the Prophet ﷺ is also evident in the sharia he brought. The teachings of Islam were not revealed to burden humanity, but to bring ease and goodness.

Islamic law removed many of the heavy burdens placed upon earlier communities and established rulings in accordance with human nature. On many occasions, the Prophet ﷺ chose the easier course so long as it involved no sin, as an expression of compassion for his ummah.

His character itself was also a visible mercy. He forgave those who harmed him, prayed for those who opposed him, and treated people with gentleness and justice.

Mercy in life and after death

The mercy of the Prophet ﷺ was not limited to the period of his earthly life. While he lived, the ummah received direct guidance, supplication, and protection from punishment. After his passing, that mercy continued through the teachings, Sunnah, and sharia he left behind.

His teachings remain guidance for all times, leading people toward goodness and salvation. Whoever follows his Sunnah receives a share of that mercy, whenever and wherever he lives.

The essence of prophetic mercy

This verse shows that the essence of the message of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is compassion, not violence; guidance, not misguidance; and salvation, not destruction. He was sent to save humanity from darkness into light, from error into guidance, and from ruin into everlasting happiness. By understanding the meaning of this verse, a believer realizes that all the teachings of the Prophet ﷺ are mercy, and that love for him is a path to the mercy of Allah. For through him, humanity comes to know its Lord, understand the purpose of life, and find the path of salvation in this world and the Hereafter.

## The Prophet as Light and Radiant Lamp

The Qur’an portrays Prophet Muhammad ﷺ not only as the bearer of a message, but also as a light that illuminates human life. This light is the light of guidance, faith, and truth that brings humanity out of the darkness of misguidance into the brightness of divine guidance. Allah ﷻ says:

> قَدْ جَاءَكُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ نُورٌ وَكِتَابٌ مُبِينٌ
>
> “There has certainly come to you from Allah a light and a clear Book.” (QS. Al-Ma’idah: 15)

Some scholars of tafsir explain that what is meant by “light” in this verse is Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, because through him humanity comes to know the path of truth. He became the means through which revelation descended, the guide toward faith, and the example in living a life pleasing to Allah.

The meaning of this light shows that before the coming of the Prophet ﷺ, humanity was in darkness. That darkness consisted of shirk, ignorance, oppression, and moral corruption. Through his message, humanity was guided toward the light of tawhid, knowledge, justice, and noble character.

Allah ﷻ also affirms the position of the Prophet ﷺ as an illuminating lamp:

> يَا أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ إِنَّا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ شَاهِدًا وَمُبَشِّرًا وَنَذِيرًا وَدَاعِيًا إِلَى اللَّهِ بِإِذْنِهِ وَسِرَاجًا مُنِيرًا
>
> “O Prophet, indeed We have sent you as a witness, a bearer of glad tidings, a warner, a caller to Allah by His permission, and an illuminating lamp.” (QS. Al-Ahzab: 45–46)

In this verse, Allah mentions several positions of the Prophet ﷺ:

- A witness over humankind.

- A bearer of glad tidings for the believers.

- A warner to those who reject the truth.

- A caller to Allah by His permission.

- A lamp that illuminates the path of life.

The expression “سِرَاجًا مُنِيرًا” (an illuminating lamp) indicates that the Prophet ﷺ did not merely carry light, but that he himself was a source of the light of guidance. Just as a lamp dispels the darkness of the night, so too did he illuminate human hearts with faith and knowledge.

This prophetic light is evident in every aspect of his life. His speech was guidance, his actions were example, and his character was a light leading humanity toward perfection.

The Qur’an also gives a portrayal of this light in the word of Allah:

اللَّهُ نُورُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ مَثَلُ نُورِهِ كَمِشْكَاةٍ فِيهَا مِصْبَاحٌ… نُورٌ عَلَى نُورٍ يَهْدِي اللَّهُ لِنُورِهِ مَنْ يَشَاءُ

“Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth. The example of His light is like a niche within which is a lamp… light upon light. Allah guides to His light whom He wills.” (QS. An-Nur: 35)

Some scholars explain that the light in this verse is also connected to the prophetic light that Allah bestowed upon Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, which became a cause of guidance for humanity.

Thus, the Prophet ﷺ is light in several meanings:

- The light of revelation that he brought.

- The light of faith that he planted in human hearts.

- The light of character that became the example for life.

- The light of guidance that saves humanity from misguidance.

Through this prophetic light, human beings come to know their Lord, understand the purpose of their life, and find the path of salvation in this world and the Hereafter. Without this light, humanity would remain in the darkness of ignorance and misguidance.

Therefore, loving the Prophet ﷺ, following his Sunnah, and taking his character as a model means walking in the light that Allah sent down for all humanity. For he is a lamp that never goes out, whose light continues to illuminate the hearts of the believers until the end of time.

## The Raising of the Prophet’s Rank and Name

Among the great honors that Allah ﷻ bestowed upon Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is the raising of his rank and the elevation of the mention of his name in this world and the Hereafter. This is affirmed in the word of Allah:

> أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ وَوَضَعْنَا عَنْكَ وِزْرَكَ الَّذِي أَنْقَضَ ظَهْرَكَ وَرَفَعْنَا لَكَ ذِكْرَكَ
>
> “Have We not expanded for you your breast, and removed from you your burden, which had weighed heavily upon your back, and raised high for you your mention?” (QS. Ash-Sharh: 1–4)

This verse is a direct declaration from Allah of the immense favor granted to the Prophet ﷺ. Allah expanded his chest, strengthened his heart, and made easy for him to carry the heavy trust of the message. The burden that once felt so heavy was lightened by the help of Allah, so that he was able to convey revelation and guide humanity with extraordinary patience and firmness.

The peak of that favor is in the word of Allah: “And raised high for you your mention.” The scholars explain that the meaning of this verse is that Allah elevated the name of the Prophet ﷺ in every corner of the earth and throughout all time. There is not a single hour of the day or night except that his name is mentioned together with the name of Allah, whether in the adhan, the shahadah, the prayer, the khutbah, or many other acts of worship. Every time a servant pronounces the testimony of tawhid, he also mentions the name of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ as the Messenger of Allah. This is an honor that was not granted to any prophet before him.

This elevation in rank is also evident in his status before Allah as the seal of the prophets and messengers, the leader of the children of Adam, and the one who will intercede on the Day of Judgment. Allah not only raised his name in this world, but also promised him the Praised Station in the Hereafter, which is the hope of all creation.

Thus, the raising of the rank of the Prophet ﷺ and the exaltation of his name are clear proof of Allah’s love for His noblest servant. It is also a reminder for the Muslim ummah that his nobility demands complete honor, love, and obedience toward the Messenger of Allah ﷺ in every aspect of life.

## Obedience to the Messenger Is Obedience to Allah

The position of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in Islam is not merely that of the conveyor of revelation, but that of the primary means by which the will of Allah is known and His command is carried out. Therefore, the Qur’an affirms that obedience to the Messenger is an inseparable part of obedience to Allah. Allah says:

> مَنْ يُطِعِ الرَّسُولَ فَقَدْ أَطَاعَ اللَّهَ
>
> “Whoever obeys the Messenger has indeed obeyed Allah.” (QS. An-Nisa’: 80)

This verse shows that the command of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ is not an independent command standing on its own, but rather an explanation and application of the command of Allah. Obedience to him means accepting revelation, following guidance, and taking his Sunnah as an example in all aspects of life. For everything he commanded came from revelation, and everything he forbade was a form of protection from destruction.

This meaning is also affirmed in the word of Allah:

> وَمَا آتَاكُمُ الرَّسُولُ فَخُذُوهُ وَمَا نَهَاكُمْ عَنْهُ فَانْتَهُوا
>
> “Whatever the Messenger gives you, take it; and whatever he forbids you, refrain from it.” (QS. Al-Hashr: 7)

This verse shows that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ holds authority in establishing rulings, explaining the sharia, and guiding the ummah in matters of religion and life. His command must be obeyed, because such obedience in reality returns to Allah.

In a hadith, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:

> مَنْ أَطَاعَنِي فَقَدْ أَطَاعَ اللَّهَ، وَمَنْ عَصَانِي فَقَدْ عَصَى اللَّهَ
>
> “Whoever obeys me has indeed obeyed Allah, and whoever disobeys me has indeed disobeyed Allah.” (HR. Bukhari and Muslim)

This hadith confirms the inseparable relationship between obedience to Allah and obedience to His Messenger. The Prophet ﷺ did not speak out of personal desire, but conveyed revelation and guidance from Allah; therefore, following him is the path to salvation.

The ulama and poets have also expressed this meaning in their poetry. Among the well-known lines is:

> مَنْ يُطِعِ الْمُخْتَارَ يَفُزْ بِرِضَا رَبِّ السَّمَاءِ وَيَسْعَدِ الدَّهْرَا

Whoever obeys the Chosen One will attain the pleasure of the Lord of heaven, and his life will be blessed through all time.

This line of poetry portrays that the path to the pleasure of Allah cannot be separated from obedience to the Prophet ﷺ. For he is the guide to the light, the one who shows the way to truth, and the perfect example in living life. The meaning of obedience to the Messenger is not merely outward compliance with commands, but also loving him, honoring his Sunnah, and making his teachings the guide of life. This obedience includes belief, speech, and action, so that a believer does not find the commands of the Messenger burdensome, because he realizes that they are in fact the commands of Allah.

Thus, these verses and hadith affirm that there is no path to obedience to Allah except through His Messenger. Whoever follows him is upon guidance, and whoever turns away from him has distanced himself from the path of salvation. Therefore, loving and obeying Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is the essence of faith and the path toward the pleasure of Allah.

### Conclusion

From the Qur’anic verses that have been mentioned, it becomes clear that Allah ﷻ Himself affirms the nobility and rank of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He is portrayed as a messenger full of compassion toward his ummah, a great favor that brought humanity out of misguidance, a mercy to all worlds, a light that illuminates the path of life, and the chosen servant whose rank and name were raised high in this world and the Hereafter. All of these portrayals show that his prophethood was not merely a task, but also a divine gift that brought salvation to humankind.

These verses also affirm that obedience to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ is an inseparable part of obedience to Allah. Through him, human beings come to know revelation, understand the sharia, and find the path toward eternal happiness. Without the message brought by the Prophet ﷺ, humanity would have remained in the darkness of misguidance and ignorance.

By understanding his nobility as portrayed in the Qur’an, the heart of a believer will be filled with love, reverence, and longing to follow his Sunnah. Love for the Prophet ﷺ is not merely an expression of the tongue, but must be manifested in obedience, reverence for his teachings, and sincere commitment to following his guidance in every aspect of life.

May Allah ﷻ make us among those who recognize the nobility of His Messenger, love him with the truest love, follow his Sunnah with full sincerity, and are gathered with him under the shade of His mercy on the Last Day. May blessings and peace always be upon Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, his family, his companions, and all those who follow his guidance until the end of time.
