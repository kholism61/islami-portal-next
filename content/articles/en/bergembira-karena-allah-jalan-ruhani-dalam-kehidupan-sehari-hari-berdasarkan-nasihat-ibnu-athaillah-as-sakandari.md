## Introduction

The counsel of Ibn 'Ata'illah al-Sakandari to his companions is filled with spiritual guidance centered on the relationship between the servant’s heart and his Lord. He does not begin with outward legal rulings, but with prayer and hope that Allah will make human happiness rest in Him alone, not in worldly pleasures or fleeting comforts. He asks that people be granted contentment with Allah, satisfaction with what comes from Him, and admission among those who understand His intention in their lives.

Those who truly know Allah are described as servants who understand that the purpose of life is to return to Him and serve Him. They know that Allah is always near, observing their movement and stillness, and managing every detail of existence. This awareness protects them from turning to creation as their ultimate source of benefit and harm.

By contrast, many people remain heedless because they are occupied with the created world and its visible attractions. Even when they worship, their worship may remain external, never penetrating the heart. The result is obedience without deep spiritual consciousness.

At the core of this counsel lies one great principle: true happiness is found only in a living relationship between the servant and Allah.

## Those Who Understand Allah

Those who understand Allah are people who know why they exist. They do not live merely to collect comfort, status, or wealth, but to journey to Allah through obedience, service, and inward sincerity. In modern life, such people still work, plan, and engage society, but the world is not their final destination.

A person who understands Allah does not attach his heart to created beings. He may earn a living, seek help, and interact with society, but his inner reliance remains upon Allah alone. Because of that, he is not shattered by financial hardship, professional failure, or social pressure. Nor is he intoxicated by success, praise, or privilege.

This awareness also affects moral conduct. Someone who knows Allah remains truthful, trustworthy, and responsible even when no human eye is watching. He does not cheat in trade, betray in employment, or manipulate others for gain, because he lives with the consciousness of divine watchfulness.

In a world driven by pressure, competition, and material standards, people who know Allah become rare sources of calm, honesty, and balance. They are not cut off from society, but they move through it with hearts anchored above it.

## Three Categories of People in Receiving Blessings

Ibn 'Ata'illah explains that people fall into three broad categories in the way they relate to Allah’s blessings.

The first group rejoices in the blessing only because of the pleasure it gives. They enjoy wealth, health, popularity, or comfort purely for their immediate sweetness, without attention to the One who granted them. In modern terms, these are people whose happiness rises and falls entirely with material success. When blessings disappear, their world collapses.

The second group rejoices in blessings because they see them as gifts from Allah. They recognize the Giver and show gratitude, yet their joy still remains tied largely to the blessing itself. This is better than the first level because it contains faith and gratitude, but the heart has not yet fully passed beyond created things.

The third and highest group rejoices in Allah alone, not in His gifts as separate objects of delight. Such a person may receive blessings, but his heart is occupied with the Giver more than the gift. He remains calm in abundance and in deprivation because his happiness is not suspended upon changing worldly states.

This teaching is especially relevant in a modern age of restless consumption. Two people may receive the same blessing: one becomes heedless, another becomes grateful, and a third is raised beyond the blessing itself into nearness to Allah.

## Happiness in Prayer

Ibn 'Ata'illah describes prayer as one of the greatest gifts Allah gives to His servants. Prayer is not merely a formal duty but a meeting between the servant and his Lord. In it, the believer stands before Allah, confesses his need, lowers himself in humility, and experiences closeness that no worldly pleasure can match.

The Messenger of Allah said that the coolness of his eyes was placed in prayer. This means that his deepest comfort and joy were found there. The more a servant knows Allah, the more sweetness he can taste in prayer. Since the Prophet’s knowledge of Allah was unmatched, his joy in prayer also surpassed that of every other human being.

Many people in modern life perform prayer only as routine. It becomes a duty completed outwardly without inward حضور القلب. As a result, prayer no longer functions as a source of light and rest. Yet when prayer is performed with awareness, reverence, and presence, it becomes a refuge from anxiety, confusion, and spiritual dryness.

One who finds happiness in prayer no longer seeks peace only through material relief. He returns to prayer when life narrows, when the heart becomes restless, and when worldly success loses its taste. In sujud he finds what wealth, power, and social approval cannot give.

## The Danger of Depending on Created Beings

One of the most subtle and destructive diseases of the heart is dependence upon created beings. Human beings often treat people, wealth, positions, or worldly means as their place of reliance, even though all of them are only instruments with no power independent of Allah.

The real problem is not using worldly means. Islam permits effort, work, medicine, trade, and cooperation. The danger lies in the heart’s attachment to those means while forgetting the Lord who created and controls them. When the heart depends on people, it is constantly unstable: happy when they give, broken when they refuse, proud when praised, crushed when criticized.

The one who knows Allah, however, uses means without worshipping them inwardly. If people help him, he sees Allah’s favor. If people deny him, he does not collapse, because he knows the true source remains unchanged. This frees the heart from slavery to opinion, position, and provision.

Modern life intensifies this disease. Many people attach their worth to career success, social status, public validation, or financial security. That attachment breeds fear, stress, and disappointment. But the heart that relies on Allah remains firm, because it rests upon the One who never changes.

## Conclusion

From Ibn 'Ata'illah’s counsel on knowing Allah, receiving blessings, experiencing the sweetness of prayer, and avoiding dependence on created things, one truth becomes clear: the essence of spiritual life is the purification of the heart until it relies on Allah alone.

Those who know Allah do not make the world the center of their happiness. They may possess worldly means, but their joy is not chained to them. They see blessings as signs, prayer as intimacy, and every state as an opportunity to draw nearer to Allah.

In an age filled with competition, anxiety, and material obsession, this teaching remains deeply relevant. Human beings continue to search for happiness outside themselves, yet remain empty because their hearts are disconnected from Allah. Real peace returns only when the heart returns to its true Lord.

Whoever finds Allah has lost nothing. And whoever loses Allah possesses nothing, even if the whole world lies in his hands.
