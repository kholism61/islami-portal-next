### Fasting and the Cultivation of the Human Soul

As part of its Ramadan scholarly programs, Al-Azhar held a fiqh gathering under the title “Bab ar-Rayyan” with the theme “Fasting and Its Effects on the Formation of the Human Soul.” The discussion stressed that fasting is not merely a ritual act of worship, but a spiritual discipline that shapes the character and ethics of the Muslim.

### Fasting and the Goal of Taqwa

The gathering emphasized that the primary purpose behind the obligation of fasting is the attainment of taqwa. Fasting trains the human being to refrain from the desire for food and marital relations from dawn until sunset, not in order to torture the body, but in order to educate the soul and accustom it to self-restraint. From this restraint is born the self-control that shapes a God-conscious person in every area of life.

### Reviving the Heart and the Conscience

One of the greatest effects of fasting is that it awakens the heart and the conscience. A believer who fasts learns to watch over himself, to avoid lying, cheating, and blameworthy behavior. Fasting does not stop at hunger and thirst. It also requires restraining the tongue from ugly speech and guarding conduct from corrupt character. For that reason, the Prophet ﷺ described fasting as a shield that protects from sin and disobedience.

### Fasting as Training in Self-Mastery

Unlike some acts of worship that call for outward activity, fasting is fundamentally an act of restraint. This is one of its great strengths. It teaches discipline, patience, and mastery over the self. Such training produces firmness of heart and strength of will, qualities that are essential in the trials of life.

### A Hidden Worship That Nurtures Sincerity

Fasting is a hidden act of worship between the servant and Allah. No one truly knows the honesty of a person's fast except Allah. For that reason, fasting plants sincerity and the awareness that Allah is always watching. When a person fasts out of faith and in hope of Allah's pleasure alone, he is building a sincere and intimate relationship with his Lord.

### Ramadan as a Season of Change

Ramadan is a month in which rewards are multiplied and the doors of goodness are opened. It is a golden opportunity to increase righteous deeds, reform the self, and renew spiritual commitment. A sound fast gives birth to a person who is gentler, more patient, and more caring toward others, so that the fruits of fasting are felt not only in the individual but also in society.

### The Wisdom of Ramadan

Fasting is a comprehensive school of the soul. It trains taqwa, revives the conscience, strengthens self-control, and implants sincerity. When these meanings take root, Ramadan truly becomes a month of personal formation that produces real change in the life of a Muslim.
